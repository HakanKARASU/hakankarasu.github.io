Project Objective

The objective of a project focused on calculating lift and drag forces for a NACA airfoil would typically be to gain a better understanding of the aerodynamic properties of the airfoil, and to develop the ability to predict its performance under different conditions. The NACA (National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics) airfoil is a popular choice for aircraft wings and other aerodynamic applications, due to its relatively simple shape and well-defined characteristics. By studying the lift and drag forces generated by the airfoil, it is possible to determine its suitability for different types of aircraft and to optimize its design for maximum performance. The project may involve creating a computational model of the airfoil using software such as PYTHON, ANSYS(ICEM-CFD), or SolidWorks, and using this model to simulate the airflow over the airfoil under various conditions such as angle of attack, airspeed, and Reynolds number. By analyzing the resulting data, it is possible to determine the lift and drag coefficients for the airfoil, and to gain insights into the underlying physics of the airflow. Overall, the goal of the project would be to develop a deeper understanding of the principles of aerodynamics, and to apply this knowledge to the design and optimization of aircraft and other aerodynamic systems.
